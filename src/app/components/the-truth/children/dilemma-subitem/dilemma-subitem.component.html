Dilemma!

The Dilemma statement is very important. It begins the 2nd transition, which is designed to help the prospect understand that we are not going to simply accept “I’ll just tell him” and that we need to get to the truth. We may not be and probably are not yet at the truth, but we have just raised a flag that we won’t settle for a flippant response to this question.

In most cases, we are not at a point where we are closing the account.
