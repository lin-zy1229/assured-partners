Why?
This is a difficult step when you are mapping out your service. Why? Because you are going to want to talk a lot and you could likely start using words like “me”, “us”, “our” and “I” – they are officially off limits. This is about the prospect and what they want, not you and what you feel you need to give them.
Our objective in this step is to be sure they understand the process of the service or exactly how it will work when they employ us to make it happen.

First you will ask them straight out:

You
“Mr. X, I want to be sure we are on the same page, so in regards to this Claims Review you say you want, can you describe for me how you want the process of getting the information, putting together the analysis and getting any issues resolved, how you want that to unfold?”

They
“This is the first time I’ve heard of this”
Or
“It’s been so long, I just don’t know”
etc.... That’s okay, that isn’t an unusual reaction.

That is when we say again:

We
“I know of a company a lot like yours, may I tell you how they wanted it done?”

At this point you have to stay in the (mode):

You
“They said they wanted this”
Or
“I know of a company a lot like yours, they’re about the same size and also in manufacturing and here’s what they said they wanted.”
You
They asked that their broker collect the loss runs and perform the review the first week of July, just like you. They also then expected the broker to provide information, such as the average duration of similar claims, if he or she found any on-going claims that needed addressing. Is this making sense so far? (sure) Okay, good.

They also wanted the broker to provide benchmarking for any claims with suspiciously high reserves or estimates – are you familiar with what “reserves” are? (Yeah, I’ve seen a report before) Super.

Then, the last thing was they wanted their broker to handle the underwriter, working on their behalf to get the estimates in line, and the on-going claims resolved, so they didn’t have to pay too much in inflated premium. Does that sound like what you are after? (It sure does)

Okay, what parts really stood out to you?
“How” – A Vessel to “Trust” I started selling a lot of insurance when I quit selling and began educating and helping people understand how things work – if they wanted to learn of course.

I learned how a fully insured health insurance renewal is calculated, then I learned a way to show it to my prospects so they could understand it. It all began with the “How” step and making sure they “got it”. It was amazing how much trust I was able to develop simply by explaining where their money was going and why.
Keep Writing! Once you finish your example, be sure to focus on your notes again. As the buyer goes through what resonated with him or her, make sure you get it down on paper – they are telling you what they want from you – don’t miss it!
When they finish their first explanation, ask them “What else” or “Did anything else stand out” and keep writing. Don’t be afraid to stop them and ask for more time to write down what they are saying. This action communicates sincerity and interest – something sales people like to verbalize instead of demonstrate.
