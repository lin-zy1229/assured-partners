Why Talking About Yourself and Your Company Doesn’t Work

Your friends have invited you and your spouse to dinner. They have also invited another couple you haven’t met. During the dinner, the new couple dominates the conversation as they talk about how their children have performed in school or athletics, etc. They talk about how successful they are in their careers. At the end of the dinner, you know much about them, but they haven’t asked many, if any, questions about you.

Think about it. When you go into a presentation with an agenda that simply regurgitates your company line you use statements like:

  • We are the “X” biggest agency in the business
    •
  • We have all these great markets
    •
  • We have vast amounts of experience
    •
  • Our service team is the best in the industry
    •
  • I have solved problems for many companies over the years.
    •

    For Discussion

  • What are your impressions of this couple?
    •
  • Would you be anxious to join them for another dinner anytime soon?
    •
  • Were you impressed by the new couple?
    •
  • Consider the reasons for your reactions . . .
  • Is the message you are delivering using any different from your competition?
    •
  • Does your message create an environment where the buyer is thinking “This is different”?
    •
  • Or would they say, “This presenter is really interested in my company and our issues”?
