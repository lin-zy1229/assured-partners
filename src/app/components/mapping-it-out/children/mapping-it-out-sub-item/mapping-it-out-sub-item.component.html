Mapping it Out Your job in this phase of the meeting is to determine what the prospect actually wants instead of your telling them what they need. You are going to listen, write and guide the prospect. You are not going to tell them what to do, unless they actually ask you to – and even then you will be careful to provide them with examples and ask them what they liked about them – again, do NOT tell them what they should like, ask them.
Write it down, shut up and listen! Even if you think you have a photographic memory, it is important to write down what you hear the prospect say. This will demonstrate that you are A. This will demonstrate that you are B. Interested in what the buyer is saying. C. Making sure you get what they WANT right!
