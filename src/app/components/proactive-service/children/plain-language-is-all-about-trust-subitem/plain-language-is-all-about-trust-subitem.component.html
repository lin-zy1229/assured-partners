Plain Language is all About TRUST
The “art” of creating wedges from our proactive services begins with understanding that the terminology we use REQUIRES we avoid insurance jargon, lingo and acronyms.
When these phrases and terms come up while writing Proactive Services, stop and ask yourself,
You
“What do I mean by ‘X’?

Come up with another way of saying it which anyone would understand.

Articulating our services concisely and with plain language is a key to winning our prospect’s trust.

Attitude – Walk Away Ability . .
To the degree that you need a prospect to buy from you is the degree you are subject to manipulation. Ask yourself before the call,

You
"If this person does not buy from me, will it change the way I eat, the clothes I wear, the car I drive, or my retirement?”

No. It will not, so go for it! – Randy Schwantz
Practice

Allow yourself the latitude to use any language you want as you are getting your thoughts on paper (so to speak). Once you feel confident you have all the information you need to effectively communicate the process, then begin to develop the language.

Read the Proactive Service out loud. Note the areas where you would modify the structure to make it flow more conversationally. Amend the areas where you are using jargon, lingo and acronyms and replace them with phrases or brief explanations.

For example, instead of saying
You
“Experience MOD”

You might say,
“The experience MOD, or the numerical value used in a work comp policy that is used to set your rates”.
It is always better to explain thing in “layman’s” terms, knowing your prospect is understanding you, than to hope they get the lingo.
