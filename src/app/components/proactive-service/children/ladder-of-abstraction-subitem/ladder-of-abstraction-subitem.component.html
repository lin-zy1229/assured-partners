1. Determine a Proactive Service
2. Place an action verb first (ex. Renewal Forecast becomes Forecasting Renewals or Loss Control becomes Controlling Losses)
3. Develop the process
  • What is needed to perform the service - almost always something tangible - reports, loss runs, policies, benefit plan designs, etc.
  • What is done with the information obtained - analysis, review, audit, etc.
  • Based on the information derived from step two, what is the plan of action, specifically, to correct the issue - negotiate with the underwrite, find better markets, develop a strategy, etc.
  • Put the information into a step by step format
    • A. Gather all the policies
    • B. Review them for coverage gaps
    • C. Contact the carrier to have the gaps filled and develop new pricing models
  • Identify the strongest step - where does the producer's expertise shine through - where's the MEAT of what we do - it is almost always step two or three
  • Break that step into the process that makes it work
    • A. Examine the exclusions
    • B. Identify which exclusions need to be amended
    • C. Market the coverage to find the best deal
  • You now have written two wedges:
    • A. When your broker came out to evaluate your coverages and they got out your policies, identified any areas where you needed more or less coverage and then developed a marketing strategy to address these issues, so you didn't have to worry about paying for coverage you don't need, or not having the coverage you do, how did that go?
    • B. When your broker came out to evaluate your coverages and they got our a list of your exclusions, identified any areas where you needed coverage or didn't need coverage, then went to the market to determine your best deal, so you didn't have to worry about not having coverage you need or paying extra for coverage you didn't need, were you satisfied with how they did that?
