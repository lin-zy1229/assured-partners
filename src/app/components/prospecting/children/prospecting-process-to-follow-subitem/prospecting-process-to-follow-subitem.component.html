Prospecting - A Process to Follow
1. Identify four verticals or geographic regions to target
2. Using your “Ideal Prospect” models, identify 20-25 target companies representing no less than $5,000 (or whatever minimum revenue threshold has been determined to best suit your situation)
3. Using Sales Navigator, Dunn & Bradstreet or other lead list building tools, identify the CEOs, CFOs, Risk Managers, Controllers, and HR Personnel for each company
4. You should then have approximately 50-60 contacts per “category” or when managing four campaigns at once, a total of approximately 200-250 contacts
5. Attempt to establish a connection via LinkedIn with these potential prospects – if successful, store those contacts and identify them as “A” contacts.
6. Develop a very short (15-30 seconds) video to be distributed for the campaign via the “PointDrive” tool. The video should be catered to the specific niche you are working and should contain a link to a “landing page” with further information (could simply be the AP website)
7. If you choose to post on LinkedIn, the posts are viewable for all your contacts. It is recommended you post 2 – 3 times per week. As a “Sales Navigator” customer, you may use Elevate (a warehouse of posting material relevant to AP and insurance) to find material.
Why post on LinkedIn?
1. Establish yourself as a thought leader with expertise
  • Industry insights, trends, functional expertise
    • 2. Add value to your network
  • Leadership, career pieces, productivity tips
    • 3. Recognize your network to build relationships
  • Colleagues’ posts, client news, tag connections
    • 4. Share why you are proud to work at your company
  • Culture, achievements, leadership perspectives
    • 5. Drive action with a clear call to action
  • Product promotion, job postings, marketing content
