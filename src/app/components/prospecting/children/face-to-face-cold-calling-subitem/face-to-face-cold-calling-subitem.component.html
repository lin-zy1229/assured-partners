Face to Face Cold Calling
1. Don’t Look Like You’re Selling
  • Ditch the tie
  • Don’t carry briefcase, notebook, etc.
    • 2. Get to Know the Receptionist
  • Make note of her name, this probably won’t be the only time you see him/her
  • Pick out something to talk about – befriend him/her
    • 3. Ask for the Person by Name
  • Be prepared, ask for “Joan, your CFO” not “Your CFO”
    • 4. If Your Contact is Busy
  • Leave your drop and move on, plan to come back
    • 5. If Your Contact Comes Out
  • Tell them you know they are busy and that you would like to get on their calendar, unless they have time now
    • 6. Be Persistent
  • It’s unlikely you’ll score on your first drop in, plan your schedule to come back no sooner than one week later and no later than three weeks later
    •
    Why Add Face to Face? Isn’t usingthe Phone More Efficient?
  • Oddly enough, in today’s world, there is no substitute for getting in front of someone
  • Push back can be high, but generally people are more receptive to a passive approach once you are in front of them – i.e., don’t force the meeting right then, ask if you can get on their calendar when it is convenient for them to meet
  • People are much less likely to cancel having met the person they have scheduled
    •
    Drops Lead to Meetings
    – Try and come up with a creative leave behind or “Drop”
    – Some examples that have had success are:
  • Yeti knock off cups “Why pay more?”
  • Rubiks Cubes “Insurance can be a tough puzzle to solve – let us help”
  • School gear (where did the person you met go to college?)
  • What other ideas do you have
    • – Leave the drop without any contact information – simply had it to the receptionist (be sure there is a note attached with the name of who it is for) and ask her/him to deliver it
    – Follow up the next day with a call or another visit and say “I’m the guy/gal who left you “X” yesterday
