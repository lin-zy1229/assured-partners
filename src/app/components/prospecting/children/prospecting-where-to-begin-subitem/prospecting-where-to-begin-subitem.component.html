Many of us, when focused on building a book of business from scratch, found ourselves falling into a common trap. We would quote anything. Any type of business and any kind of risk. We had no parameters or standards upon which we would lean to ensure we were making the most efficient use of our time.

Objective: To determine what characteristics our best opportunities possess, then to define those and structure our prospecting approach around those “ideal” parameters.

Let’s take a moment to define your ideal prospect. Don’t worry, your definition will probably change several times throughout your career . . . what is most important is that we get used to writing down our objectives and working with a plan!
